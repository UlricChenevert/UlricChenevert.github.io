Premise

Artificial gravity fields work on artificial mass, which can be used for a myriad of uses, including but not limited to anti-character shields, launch tubes, gravity cannons, and of course, Gravity Drives.

Gravity Drives consist of a mass block and a gravity generator on the same grid, which creates a “reactionless drive” with monstrous acceleration at a large PCU cost and design complexity.

Drive Cost

• Simple: 1 mass generator : 1 gravity generator = 210 PCU (0.68x large hydrogen thrust)
• Server PCU optimized: 32 mass generators : 1 gravity generator = 1k PCU (21.8x large hydrogen thrust)

Integrating the Drive with WASD

Core Events:
1. Press key (W, A, S, D)
2. Influences controllable Object (Thruster, Wheels)
3. Detect Change (Event controllers, Scripts)
4. Drive Control (Control thrust, Control Stop)

Multiplayer / Scriptless Logic

Detect 100% thrust: Use an Event Controller. WASD input creates a detectable 100% thrust for a specific direction.

Ensure 9.8 m/s: 20x increase acceleration commands to gravity generator group from a timer block. Each command shifts by 1 m/s² to cover the range. One array per direction simplifies setup.

The >5m/s Threshold: If speed is less than 5m/s, turn off gravity blocks; else, turn on. This prevents infinite loops of thrust over-compensation caused by inertia dampeners. Basic thrusters handle the low-speed maneuvering.

Visuals: If acceleration is less than 0, stop artificial mass. Not strictly necessary, but looks incredible in action.

Engineering Tips

• Null Acceleration: If blocks are on but nothing is happening, check if the opposite side is counteracting the field.
• Prototyping: Test on a small, one-direction craft before integrating into a capital ship.
• Debugging: Use an antenna while building to verify your groups are firing correctly.
• Safety First: Check the gravity field direction twice before powering the artificial mass blocks, or you'll meet the hangar wall very quickly.
